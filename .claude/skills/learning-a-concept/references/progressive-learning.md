Structure for teaching concepts, methodologies, and mental models in digestible levels.

## The Five Levels

### Level 1: Overview & Motivation

**Goal**: Understand WHAT and WHY

Cover:

- What problem does this concept address?
- What existed before? Why is this approach better?
- Who created it and what was the context?
- Who benefits from it? In what situations?
- When should you NOT use it?

### Level 2: Core Principles

**Goal**: Understand the foundational ideas

Cover:

- 3-5 fundamental principles or pillars
- How they relate to each other
- One real-world analogy per principle
- Common misunderstandings for each

### Level 3: The Methodology

**Goal**: Understand the process or system

Cover:

- Step-by-step breakdown of the methodology
- How the principles translate into practice
- Decision points and how to handle them
- Variations or schools of thought

### Level 4: Practical Application

**Goal**: Start applying it in your own context

Cover:

- Getting started checklist
- Recommended tools or setups
- Common starter templates or workflows
- Mistakes beginners make and how to avoid them
- Example: walk through a realistic scenario end-to-end

### Level 5: Next Steps

**Goal**: Know where to go deeper

Cover:

- Advanced topics to explore
- Best books, courses, and resources for each
- Community resources and forums
- How to get help
- Propose a mini-project or hands-on exercise to solidify understanding

## Template Principles

- Every template should be immediately usable (fillable, printable, or copyable)
- Start with the simplest version, add complexity incrementally
- Include brief instructions on how and when to use each template
- Show a filled-in example alongside the blank template
