# Progressive Learning Framework — Paper Deep Dive

Structure for understanding a specific academic paper in depth.

## The Five Levels

### Level 1: Background & Motivation

**Goal**: Understand why this paper exists

Cover:

- What problem does this paper address?
- What was the state of the art before this paper?
- What gap or limitation in prior work motivated this research?
- Who are the authors and what is their research context?
- Why is this paper significant or worth reading?

### Level 2: Core Method

**Goal**: Understand what the paper actually proposes

Cover:

- The main idea or contribution in plain language
- Technical approach broken down step by step
- Key equations or algorithms explained intuitively
- Architecture diagrams or visual explanations
- What assumptions does the method make?
- How does it differ from prior approaches?

### Level 3: Key Results

**Goal**: Know what the paper achieved

Cover:

- Main experimental results and metrics
- Datasets and benchmarks used
- How results compare to baselines and prior work
- Ablation studies — which components matter most
- Limitations acknowledged by the authors
- Any surprising or counterintuitive findings

### Level 4: Practical Implications

**Goal**: Extract what practitioners can use

Cover:

- What can you actually do with this paper's contributions?
- Available implementations (official repo, community ports)
- How to reproduce or build on the results
- When would you use this approach vs. alternatives?
- Computational requirements and practical constraints
- Tips from the community on applying this work

### Level 5: Related Work & Next Steps

**Goal**: Place the paper in its broader context

Cover:

- Papers this work builds on (key references to read first)
- Papers that build on this work (important follow-ups)
- Competing approaches published around the same time
- Open questions the paper raises
- Suggested reading order for understanding the full lineage
- Active research directions spawned by this paper
